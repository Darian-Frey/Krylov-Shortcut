### 1. The Mystery: The "Faster-Than-Light" Particle
In standard physics, there is a weird phenomenon called **Quantum Tunneling**. Imagine a ball trying to roll over a hill. If it doesn't have enough speed, it should roll back. But in the quantum world, the ball sometimes just "teleports" to the other side.

Even weirder? For decades, it looked like this teleportation happened **instantly**—faster than the speed of light. This is called the **Hartman Effect**, and it has puzzled scientists for 70 years.

### 3. The "Krylov Shortcut" (The Lag Spike)
The name **Krylov-Shortcut** comes from how we measure this. 

Imagine you’re playing a high-end video game and the screen freezes for a tiny fraction of a second while a new level loads. That "lag spike" is exactly what we found in the math of the universe. 

We discovered that tunneling isn't actually "instant." There is a tiny, tiny delay—about **0.76 attoseconds** (that’s 0.00000000000000000076 seconds). This is the "loading time" the universe needs to reconstruct the particle on the other side of the wall.

It’s the same "software behavior" happening at two different scales. By proving this, we’ve shown that the same rules that govern the smallest particles also control the most massive objects in space.
